Personal story speech

My name is Caleb Perez, I am from Playa del Carmen, Mexico, and I am currently 23 years old, turning 24 very soon. If I had to describe myself in a simple way, I would say I am someone who has always been driven by growth, challenge, and the desire to build real things. Since I was younger, I have always felt naturally attracted to technology, creativity, and understanding how systems work. Even before fully realizing it, I already enjoyed exploring digital tools, new technologies, and ideas that could become something useful in the real world.

After high school, my path was not completely linear, but that is actually one of the things that has shaped me the most. I began university studying Finance and Accounting. At that stage, I was still exploring what truly fit me, and although I found some parts interesting, I did not feel deeply engaged. It was also during the COVID period, which made the experience feel even more disconnected and less challenging than I wanted from my education.

Because I have always liked challenge and problem-solving, and because many people around me, especially teachers, had always told me I was strong in mathematics, I decided to make a change and move into Civil Engineering. At first, that decision felt exciting, because engineering gave me the challenge I was looking for. I really enjoyed the logic, the structure, and the mindset of solving problems through systems. However, as I went deeper into it, I realized that what truly interested me was not construction itself, but technology. That was an important moment for me, because it helped me understand that I was not just looking for difficulty, I was looking for the right kind of challenge.

That realization led me to switch again, this time into Information Technology and Digital Business Engineering, which felt much closer to who I really am. That was the point where things became much clearer for me. I recognized that software, systems, and technology were not just interests, but the area where I felt the most curious, motivated, and engaged. Looking back, I can even connect that to moments from my childhood. I remember being around 12 years old and building a small website project for school just because I thought it would be a creative and interesting way to do an assignment. At that age, I did not fully understand the significance of what I was doing, but I remember the excitement of creating something digital from scratch.

Later on, I also had the chance to go to the United States and explore academic options there, because I was still searching for an educational environment that truly matched the level of challenge and real-world orientation I wanted. During that period, I continued learning through online programs and opportunities such as courses from Google and Oracle. Those experiences strengthened something important in me: the confidence that I am capable of learning independently and moving forward on my own when I am truly committed to something.

After that, one of the most transformative experiences of my life came when I traveled to New Zealand for a year. Living there on my own, working, adapting, and building a life from zero in a completely different country gave me a level of independence and maturity that no classroom alone could have given me. That experience taught me how to handle uncertainty, how to trust myself, and how to keep moving forward even when everything around me is unfamiliar. It also reinforced my entrepreneurial mindset, because while I was there, I started developing one of my own business ideas. That showed me that I do not just like learning, I like building.

When I returned to Mexico, I kept reflecting on what kind of work I wanted to dedicate myself to long term. That is when a much bigger opportunity became clear to me. For more than ten years, my mother has been involved in the travel agency business, so I have grown up seeing the real operational side of that world: the customer service, the coordination, the issues that come with planning trips, and the constant friction that travelers and agencies often face. Through that, I realized there was a real problem worth solving.

That is what led me to begin developing my current project: an AI-powered travel platform connected to the vision of building a modern travel business. The idea is to improve the way people manage complete travel plans, including flights, accommodation, transportation, and activities, all in one place. What makes it especially meaningful to me is that it combines two worlds that are very real in my life: the travel industry knowledge I have been exposed to for years, and my growing commitment to software and artificial intelligence. The vision is not just to make another platform, but to create a smarter, more supportive travel experience where an AI assistant can help users manage their plans, reservations, changes, and support needs in a much more personalized and efficient way.

Alongside that, I have also had smaller entrepreneurial experiences, such as a local e-commerce business selling minimalist chairs, which taught me early lessons about logistics, margins, customer needs, and execution. Even though it was a simpler venture, it was an important part of my development because it showed me that I enjoy turning ideas into something real and testing them in the market.

When I look at everything from after high school until now, I do not see confusion. I see exploration, self-discovery, resilience, and progress. Each stage helped me understand myself better. Finance showed me what did not fully engage me. Engineering showed me I wanted challenge. Technology showed me where I truly belong. Traveling alone taught me independence. Building projects taught me initiative. Entrepreneurship taught me ownership. And my current startup journey is teaching me how to connect vision, technology, and real-world needs into something meaningful.

Today, I know much more clearly who I am than I did a few years ago. I am someone who learns by doing, who is willing to take risks to find the right path, and who wants to build real solutions with technology and artificial intelligence. More than anything, I know that I am not looking for a conventional path. I am looking for an environment where I can keep growing, building, and surrounding myself with people who are also ambitious, practical, and driven to create impact.
